My Research Projects and Publications

My academic journey has been enriched by various research projects and publications that demonstrate my dedication to advancing knowledge and exploring interdisciplinary topics. Below is a summary of my key research work and ongoing projects.

Publications

Survey Article on Hopfield Networks

Status: In review for potential publication in "La Gazette de la Société Mathématique de France".

Description: This survey examines the mathematical underpinnings and applications of Hopfield Networks, focusing on their capacity for pattern recognition and memory modeling within the framework of artificial intelligence. This work bridges theoretical mathematics and machine learning, reflecting my interest in interdisciplinary research that connects mathematics with computer science.

Research Projects

Phase Transitions in the Ising Model

Institution: Université Paris-Est Créteil (UPEC)

Description: During my research internship, I studied the phase transitions in the nearest-neighbor Ising model without an external magnetic field. I demonstrated the absence of phase transitions in one dimension and proved their existence in two dimensions using the Peierls argument. This project involved applying mathematical rigor to statistical mechanics, exploring concepts like the thermodynamic limit, Griffiths inequalities, and the evolution of spin correlation with temperature.

Machine Learning Framework for Commercial Paper Valuation

Institution: BRED Banque Populaire

Role: Data Scientist Intern – Sales Trader Assistant

Description: Collaborated with a PhD-level data scientist to refine the mathematical basis of a machine learning model for commercial paper valuation. My contributions included proposing a relevant set of mathematical notations, reviewing the proper definition of mathematical objects, and co-authoring a research paper. This project allowed me to merge theoretical knowledge with practical data science applications and exposed me to academic writing and peer-reviewed publication processes.

Expected Project on Graph Neural Networks (GNNs)

Institution: CentraleSupélec (Expected)

Focus: Research on the properties and applications of GNNs as part of machine learning projects, which will leverage my background in graph theory and topological methods. This research will expand on my previous studies and contribute to my understanding of advanced computational techniques in data analysis.
