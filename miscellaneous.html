Philosophy

My love for philosophy began in twelfth grade, sparked by an inspiring lecture from Dr. Stéphane Floccari, who emphasized the importance of daily reflection. This led me to deeper exploration during my first year of CPGE, where I encountered Nietzsche. His ideas resonated profoundly, revealing that philosophical reflection had been part of my thinking since childhood. Over time, I’ve explored philosophies such as Greek pantheism and reflected on contrasting views like Rousseau’s interpretation of the ancient Chinese Theory of Good Human Nature. Surprisingly, anime has influenced my journey as well—characters like Monkey D. Luffy from One Piece inspire me daily with their ideals of liberty and authenticity.

Classical Architecture

My fascination with classical architecture started with the awe-inspiring columns of The French Panthéon. This initial exposure evolved into a lasting interest in classical and neoclassical architecture, celebrated for its timeless elegance and intellectual gravitas.

The Art of Tea

Tea is not just a beverage to me; it represents culture, tradition, and tranquility. My passion for tea has taken me through its rich traditions—from cultivation and transformation to preparation and tasting. One of my aspirations is to open a tea shop in my later years, where I can share this passion and create a space for reflection and community.

Urban Culture

Growing up in the Paris suburbs as a mixed-race individual, I was deeply influenced by urban culture. Music, film, and sports shaped my experiences, with icons like 2Pac, Eddie Murphy, and LeBron James becoming influential figures in my life. Visual media such as The Fresh Prince of Bel-Air and The Pursuit of Happyness have further deepened my understanding of diverse perspectives, emphasizing the power of storytelling and resilience.
